Ex Origine — Core Project Definition (v0.1)
Document Type: Vision + Direction
OMA Layer Alignment: A1 (Foundational Intent) → informs A2–A5


1. Project Statement

Ex Origine is a modular real-time simulation framework that models the emergence of complexity from first principles.

It is not simply a game—it is a system where physics, information, and decision-making co-evolve, allowing players to guide, observe, or influence the unfolding of structured reality from its most primitive states.

The project is built on the Orijen Mathematical Architecture (OMA), ensuring that all mechanics—energy, matter, behavior, and intelligence—derive from consistent, traceable rules rather than arbitrary game systems.

At its core, Ex Origine answers a single question:

What happens when a world is allowed to become?


2. Defining Goals

2.1 Foundational Goals (A1–A2 Alignment)

- Emergence over scripting
  Systems must produce outcomes through interaction, not predefined events.

- Consistency across scales
  The same underlying rules apply from particle-level simulation to macro structures.

- Traceable causality
  Every state change should be explainable through prior conditions and system rules.

- Modularity by design
  Every system exists as an independent, replaceable module.


2.2 Simulation Goals (A2–A3 Alignment)

- Energy as the universal currency
  All processes consume and transform energy.

- Information as structure
  Patterns, memory, and intelligence emerge from information persistence.

- Dynamic equilibrium
  Systems stabilize and destabilize naturally.

- Non-linear evolution
  Small changes can produce large consequences.


2.3 Player Interaction Goals (A3–A4 Alignment)

- Indirect control over direct command
  The player influences systems rather than micromanaging units.

- Multiple modes of engagement
  Observer (watch)
  Influencer (nudge)
  Architect (modify rules)

- Meaningful intervention
  Player actions ripple through systems.


2.4 Technical Goals (A4–A5 Alignment)

- Deterministic simulation core (when required)
- Scalable performance architecture
- Versioned system evolution
- Data-first design


3. Narrative Vision Statement

Ex Origine is not a story that is told.
It is a story that forms.

There is no predetermined civilization, no scripted conflict, no guaranteed outcome.
Instead, there is a beginning—simple, quiet, almost empty.

From that beginning:

Energy begins to move.
Patterns begin to repeat.
Structures begin to persist.

What emerges may resemble life.
Or it may not.

The player is not a ruler, but a presence—between observer and catalyst.

Over time, the world may:
- Organize
- Collapse
- Develop intelligence
- Drift without meaning

The story is emergence itself.

Not what is designed, but what becomes inevitable.


4. Design Pillars

- Everything evolves
